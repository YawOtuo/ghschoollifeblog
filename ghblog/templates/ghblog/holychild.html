Life in Holy Child Secondary School

Priding itself among the most prestigious, religious and dedicated first choice schools in Ghana, Holy Child Senior High School has proven to be on top of its game when it comes to educating and training the girl child to be responsible and competent.

Located at …, Holy Child has been shaping lives for … years

What is life like there though? Here is the story of one Medical Doctor who attended there, Dr. Akosua Kyerewaa Amoako Atta.

Holy Child is a beautiful school. The buildings in holico are very cute, nice and attractive. They are especially neater during times of visiting of something of a similar sort. I wouldn’t describe my life there as a bed of roses but it was indeed a significant part of my life from which I have learnt some priceless lessons and experiences. The school is well equipped with beautiful facilities, like a library and some laboratories. Entertainment and Jams make the school a particularly fun place to be in. Dedicated and well-versed teachers play a role in helping students comprehend difficult concepts and ideas.

Boarding life was well, to put it nicely, horrible for me. I cried a lot. I was never really happy, always stressed here and there. My first compound duty was to scrub the bathhouse, which is not a good place to start with. Such wicked seniors I had.

My daily routine involved waking up at by 5 o’clock, preparing for mass, packing for school… Class began at 7 58, a short 10 minutes break at 10 15, a lunch break at 2 20. Then class continue till around 4 and should there be extension classes, 5 00. Dinner at 5 30. Prepping till 9 30 and sometimes extended to 12. And the cycle begins again.

My classmates were very united, fun, amazing and smart. One time the school, organized a beauty pageant and I placed fourth. It is one of the fond memories I have of the school when my shoe got stolen.

The best part of the school is how it helped to shape me spiritually. Mass was very fun and insightful. I made good friends and other friends and all in all I learnt that your enemy can truly be your best friend.
